3D Scans

Scan it. Stitch it. Print it.

Two projects that start the same way — pointing a scanner at something real — and end very differently: a backyard fence built around existing plumbing, and a miniature statue scanned, stitched, and reprinted from scratch.

Family Project

Pool Equipment Fence with Pipe Avoidance

My parents' new house came with pool equipment sitting in plain view, and they wanted it screened off without disturbing anything underground. I scanned the existing foundation and modeled the known pipe runs so we could lay out fence post locations that looked evenly spaced from every angle — without drilling into anything we shouldn't.

Once the layout was dialed in on screen, my dad and I built the fence ourselves and added shrubbery around it afterward to finish the look.

Favorite Project

Hokiebird 3D Scan to Statue

If you've spent any time around Virginia Tech, you've seen the Hokie statues that sit outside local restaurants and shops. My parents love this mascot, so I scanned one as a gift.

The scanning software kept losing tracking partway through, so I ended up with five or six separate scans instead of one clean pass. I brought all of them into Blender and manually stitched and smoothed the pieces back into a single statue. After a 36-hour print, I had an 8" replica — my personal favorite project, and one I've since reprinted and given to more than 40 friends and family.
