BOLT Electric Race Motorcycle · Oct 2022–May 2026

Chassis Design, Fairings & Team President

I joined Virginia Tech's BOLT electric motorcycle race team as a chassis engineer, designing and simulating the Emrax 248 (400kW) motor mount and switching its material from steel to aluminum for 6 lbs of weight savings. Alongside the chassis work, I took on primary ownership of BOLT VI's bodywork — modeling the fairings in CAD, splitting them into printable sections, and carrying each panel through 3D printing, wet carbon fiber layup, bonding, and paint. My senior year I stepped into the Admin Lead (President) role, leading a team of 100+ engineers with a mid-five-figure build budget and growing membership from 55 to 120.

ESP32 · RGB LED Matrix

Wi-Fi Live Stock Ticker

Built for a friend studying finance, this display pairs two 64x32 RGB LED matrices with an ESP32 to show live stock prices, the date, and the time. It pulls ticker data from the FMP API, and a simple local web server lets you change which of up to 10 tickers are shown. This was my first real dive into microcontrollers and a deliberate departure from mechanical-only projects.

Family Project

Pool Equipment Fence with Pipe Avoidance

My parents' new house came with pool equipment sitting in plain view, and they wanted it screened off without disturbing anything underground. I scanned the existing foundation and modeled the known pipe runs so we could lay out fence post locations that looked evenly spaced from every angle — without drilling into anything we shouldn't.

Once the layout was dialed in on screen, my dad and I built the fence ourselves and added shrubbery around it afterward to finish the look.

Favorite Project

Hokiebird 3D Scan to Statue

If you've spent any time around Virginia Tech, you've seen the Hokie statues that sit outside local restaurants and shops. My parents love this mascot, so I scanned one as a gift.

The scanning software kept losing tracking partway through, so I ended up with five or six separate scans instead of one clean pass. I brought all of them into Blender and manually stitched and smoothed the pieces back into a single statue. After a 36-hour print, I had an 8" replica — my personal favorite project, and one I've since reprinted and given to more than 40 friends and family.

Original Design · RC / Aerospace

RC Cessna 172 — 3D Printed Airplane

Most RC builds start from a kit; I wanted to design my own, loosely based on the Cessna 172. The current version skips yaw control deliberately — my first design, proving the rest of the airframe works before adding complexity. I estimated center of gravity, lift force, and takeoff speed, and the CAD CG lines up well with the physical model in the X/Y plane, though the 3D-printed build shifted things slightly on the Z axis.

7th Grade

1994 Anniversary Casting

My parents got married in 1994. For their anniversary, my dad and I built a backyard aluminum foundry from scratch — a custom-made forge and burner — and poured molten aluminum into a negative sand mold shaped from a 3D print I designed. The result was a solid aluminum piece representing that year, gifted to my mom.
