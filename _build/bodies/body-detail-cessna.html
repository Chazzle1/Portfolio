3D Printed RC Cessna 172

SolidWorks FDM Printing RC Flight Aerodynamics CG Analysis Servo Control

Why Design One from Scratch?

When I got interested in RC planes, most builds start from a kit — a known-good airframe someone else already designed, tested, and packaged up. I wanted something harder. I based this design on the Cessna 172, one of the most widely flown aircraft in the world, and modeled every component from the ground up in SolidWorks.

The school year has a habit of pushing the timeline out, but as of early 2024 the airframe is nearly complete. The first version deliberately omits yaw control — no rudder servo. The goal is to prove the rest of the airframe can fly before adding more complexity.

The Engineering Behind It

I built a full mass budget for the plane in a spreadsheet: motor, prop, ESC, battery, receiver, servos, and the printed structure itself. Tracking each component's actual weight let me estimate the center of gravity before committing to final assembly.

From the mass model, I estimated wing loading, predicted lift at various airspeeds using a simplified lift equation, and calculated a conservative stall and takeoff speed. Some aerodynamic constants are estimates where direct measurement isn't practical at this scale, but the numbers are consistent with comparable RC aircraft.

One known problem to solve before first flight: the CAD center of gravity matches the physical model well in X and Y but drifts noticeably on the Z axis. 3D-printed infill isn't perfectly uniform, and the internal fill pattern distributes mass differently than the solid CAD model predicts. Ballast placement before flying will address this.

What’s Left

The ailerons and elevator are built and connected. Servo travel has been dialed in and both surfaces deflect through the full range without binding. Remaining work before a first flight attempt: CG ballast, final battery placement for trim, and field range-check of the radio link. Once the baseline airframe is proven to fly, a rudder servo will be the next addition.
